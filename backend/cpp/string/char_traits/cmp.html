DR Applied to Behavior as published Correct behavior LWG 467 C++98 for std::char_traits, the semantics of eq() and lt()are the same as the built-in == and < on char respectively[1] changed to built-in == and< on unsigned char ↑ Most implementations call std::memcmp() for efficiency, which interprets the data as arrays of unsigned char. If char is signed on such implementations, std::char_traits fails to satisfy the requirements of CharTraits.
