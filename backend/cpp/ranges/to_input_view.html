input_range-only and not a common_range. 2) RangeAdaptorObject. Let e be a subexpression, and let T be decltype(e). Then the expression views::to_input(e) is expression-equivalent to: views::all(e), if it is a well-formed expression and all of the conditions below are true: T models input_range, T does not satisfy common_range, and T does not satisfy forward_range. to_input_view{e} otherwise. Consequently, to_input_view never models range concepts that are stronger than input_range. In particular, it does not model all of these: forward_range bidirectional_range random_access_range contiguous_range Additionally, to_input_view does not model common_range. However, it does model borrowed_range, constant_range, and sized_range when the underlying view V models respective concepts.
