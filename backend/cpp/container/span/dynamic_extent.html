It is being used in several contexts: To differentiate std::span of static and dynamic extent. To indicate that the extent at a certain rank index will be stored dynamically in std::extents. (since C++23) To indicate that the padded layouts for std::mdspan will dynamically store its padding value. (since C++26) [edit] Note Since std::size_t is an unsigned type, an equivalent definition is: inline constexpr std::size_t dynamic_extent = -1; See integral conversions.
