As a result, when testing std::counted_iterator against an iterator concept (e.g. forward_iterator), the determination of /*ITER_CONCEPT*/ does not take I::iterator_concept into account, and thus std::counted_iterator sometimes erroneously behaves as if it cannot model that concept. This incorrect behavior is implemented in libstdc++ prior to 10.4, and in MSVC STL prior to VS 2022 17.0 Preview 3. The standard library provides partial specializations of std::iterator_traits for pointer types, std::counted_iterator, and std::common_iterator.
