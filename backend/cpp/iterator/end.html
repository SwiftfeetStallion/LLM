(since C++20) [edit] Notes The non-array overloads exactly reflect the behavior of C::end(). Their effects may be surprising if the member function does not have a reasonable implementation. std::cend is introduced for unification of member and non-member range accesses. See also LWG issue 2128. If C is a shallow-const view, std::cend may return a mutable iterator. Such behavior is unexpected for some users. See also P2276 and P2278.
